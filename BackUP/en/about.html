About

The Canadian Undergraduate Mathematics Conference (CUMC) is Canada's premier conference for undergraduate students interested in mathematics and related fields (statistics, physics, computer science, economics, etc). Beginning in 1994 at McGill, the CUMC has grown over the last 17 years to be one of North America's largest undergraduate conferences.

The conference aims to give students of all abilities a valuable experience in mathematics beyond what is available in their normal studies. Students get to spend a few days with other students who share their passion for mathematics. In this time they get the valuable opportunity to practice giving mathematical talks on a topic of their choice, encounter ideas from areas of mathematics outside of their expertise, listen to renowned keynote speakers from a variety of disciplines and see a new Canadian mathematics department at the hosting university. The keynote speakers invited each year are either prominent figures or rising stars in their fields, and most importantly they care about undergraduates and their exposure to mathematics. In recent years, the conference has been featured internationally with students from the United States, Europe and South Asia traveling to attend.

Hosting

CUMC cannot continue without a host for each year, so an important tradition is that the students attending each conference decide at which Canadian university the next CUMC is to be held. We strongly encourage you to consider making a bid to host CUMC 2013 at your school. First and foremost, please check with your home department to get approval for hosting! The bidding process involves giving a presentation about your university and why it should host the CUMC. In the end, each attending university will have one vote to cast amongst the bidders. Please contact us with any questions.
